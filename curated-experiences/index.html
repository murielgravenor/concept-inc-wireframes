What this is

For clients who want a show,
not just a setup.

Curated Experiences is a distinct offering within Concept Inc. — built for events that require artistic direction alongside technical production. If you need a choreographer, a speciality act, a show director, or an immersive environment that audiences step into rather than sit in front of, this is where you start.

We come from a performance background. We understand a room's energy — how it changes when the lights drop, when a silence opens up, when a performer holds a moment. That instinct doesn't come from a technical spec sheet.

Most production companies can put up a rig. Fewer can tell you where it should go, how it should move, and what it should make people feel. We can do both — because the technical and the creative inform each other from the moment the idea starts. We spec lighting by thinking about the performer first. We design stage configurations with the audience's sightlines already in mind.

The result is production that holds the room — not just fills it.

Capabilities

What we can build for you.

Choreography

Movement direction for performers, acts, and branded experiences. We work with choreographers or provide direction ourselves on simpler briefs — always in service of the moment, not the moves.

Dance & performance

Live dance and performance integration into event programmes — ceremony reveals, entertainment segments, immersive walkaround acts, or full show-stoppers that anchor an evening.

Speciality acts

Acrobats, aerialists, fire performers, illusionists, contortionists, stilt walkers. We source, brief, and integrate acts into the full production — technically and creatively.

Immersive lighting design

Beyond illumination — lighting that creates environment, guides emotion, and makes a space feel intentionally built. Automated sequences, colour narrative, and atmospheric layering.

Projection mapping

Surface-mapped visuals onto architecture, objects, or custom stage elements. Custom content creation or integration of third-party content into a live production workflow.

Show direction

End-to-end creative and technical direction for the full show arc — from concept through to the final curtain. One director across every discipline.

[Client name] — Immersive Brand Launch

A brand launch that combined aerial performance, surface projection mapping on a curved architectural wall, and a custom composed sound score. Three hundred guests. A 45-minute show arc. No screens, no MC, no holding music — the production was the programme.

The brief was "make it feel like a world premiere." We took twelve weeks to build it: choreography workshops, bespoke content production, technical rehearsals in the venue, and a full dress run the night before. Every lighting cue and video transition was triggered live by the show director. Nothing was pre-timed, nothing was left to chance.

Impact

Guests were still talking about it six months later. Social media engagement increased 40% in the 48 hours following the event. Three brand partnerships were signed the following week. The client has run a Curated Experience every year since.

The process

How a Curated Experience
comes together.

This engagement is different from a standard production brief. We start with a conversation, not a spec. The creative leads — the technical follows.

01 — Enquiry

Concept conversation

Tell us the feeling, the occasion, the audience. We don't need a brief — we'll help you build one. This conversation is free and carries no obligation.

02 — Creative

Creative direction

We develop a concept document: the show arc, the creative devices, the production framework. You review and approve it before we spec a single piece of equipment or book a single act.

03 — Build

Design & build

Creative and technical design locked together. We cast, choreograph, spec equipment, and build the production schedule. Rehearsals are built into the timeline, not added at the end.

04 — Delivery

Show delivery

Technical runthrough, full dress rehearsal, performance. The show director is on the floor and at the desk — often the same person. Nothing is delegated to chance.
