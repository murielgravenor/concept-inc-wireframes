Concept Inc. was built by two people who came up through live performance before they ever touched a lighting desk. That order of things matters — it changes what you pay attention to.

The founders

Portrait placeholder — Duane

Duane [Surname]

Co-founder · Production Director

I've been in live production for over twenty years — sound engineering, touring rigs, festival stages. The kind of work where you learn that no brief survives contact with a venue, and you'd better be comfortable making decisions quickly.

Concept Inc. started from a simple observation: clients didn't need equipment rental. They needed someone who could look at a brief, look at a venue, and tell them exactly what would work — and then be there to run it. We built a company that does that.

My job on every event is to make sure the technical side is invisible. Not because it isn't there, but because it's working.

Portrait placeholder — Gareth

My background is performance — dance, physical theatre, live shows. I came into production from the audience's side, which means I've always thought about what people feel before I think about what we're running.

That instinct changes how you spec a show. The lighting rig isn't there to illuminate the room — it's there to tell people what to feel at each moment. The sound system isn't there to be loud — it's there to hold the space.

I lead the Curated Experiences side of what we do. For those projects, the production and the creative aren't two separate briefs. They're the same brief.

How we think

What we believe about production.

01

We solve problems on the spot.

Production is live. Venues change, timelines compress, gear fails. We don't escalate these to the client — we have contingencies, we adapt, and we tell you afterwards if it's something you need to know. Your event keeps moving.

02

We curate, not just supply.

Anyone can deliver equipment. We think about whether the equipment is right for the room, the brief, and the audience. If a simpler rig will serve you better, we'll tell you. If you need more than you've asked for, we'll say that too.

03

Every event is its own concept.

We don't template our thinking. A year-end function in a wine farm has a completely different production logic to a conference in a hotel ballroom. We start from the brief, not from a standard spec.

04

We stay on for the show.

The same people who took your brief and designed your production are there on the day, operating it. We don't hand off at load-in. If something needs calling in the moment, the person making that call knows your event.
