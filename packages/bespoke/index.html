When bespoke makes sense

Some events need to be designed.

Festivals & touring shows

Multi-stage builds, outdoor line arrays, touring rigs with changing venues, or any production that requires engineering from the ground up rather than a fixed spec.

Multi-day events

Conferences, festivals, or experiences running over two or more days with different setups each day — production that needs to flex within a single logistical framework.

Unusual venues

No fixed infrastructure, awkward acoustics, outdoor sites, or architecturally complex spaces. A warehouse, rooftop, beach, wine farm, or game reserve — we spec for the actual space.

Creative & performance briefs

Choreography, live performance, immersive experience, or anything that needs production to be part of the creative — not bolted on afterwards.

Process

How we build a bespoke brief.

01 — Enquiry

Tell us what you're planning

Fill in the form below with as much or as little as you have. A rough idea is enough to start — we'll ask the right questions to fill the gaps.

02 — Brief & design

We design and quote

We take your brief and come back with a full production proposal — equipment list, crew, schedule, and itemised pricing. No guesswork, no vague line items.

03 — Approval

You approve

Review the proposal, ask questions, adjust scope. We don't start production until you're satisfied with exactly what's been quoted and what it costs.

04 — Production

We produce

Full crew on site, on brief, on schedule. Load-in, build, rehearsal, event day, and packdown. One point of contact throughout.

What helps

What we'll need from you.

You don't need a full technical brief to start. These four things give us enough to build a first-pass proposal — everything else we can work out together.

01

Date

Even approximate — month and year is enough to check availability and flag any constraints.

02

Venue

If you've confirmed a venue, the space tells us a lot. If not, shortlist or location type works.

03

Approximate guest count

Determines scale of the rig. A rough number is fine — 50, 300, or "around 1,000" all give us what we need.

04

What you want guests to feel

The experience you're building towards. Not a technical spec — just the feeling, the moment, the intention.
